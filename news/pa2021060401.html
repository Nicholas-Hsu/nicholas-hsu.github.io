Paper: Biased supervision

Authors: Josse Delfgaauw; Michiel Souverijn
Published Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization (Vol. 130, Jul. 2016)
Links of the Original Papers: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article…

Abstract: Organizations can use subjective performance pay when verifiable performance measures are imperfect. However, this gives supervisors the power to direct employees toward tasks that mainly benefit the supervisor rather than the organization. We cast a principal–supervisor–agent model in a multitask setting, where the supervisor has an intrinsic preference toward specific tasks and may receive soft information on the agent’s efforts. We show that subjective performance pay based on evaluation by a biased supervisor has the same distorting effect on the agent’s effort allocation across tasks as incentive pay based on an incongruent performance measure. Combining incongruent performance measures with biased supervision can mitigate, but does not always eliminate this distortion. We apply our results to the choice between specialist and generalist middle managers, where a trade-off between monitoring ability and bias arises.

论文：《有偏的监督》*

作者：Josse Delfgaauw; Michiel Souverijn
发表杂志：《Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization》 (Vol. 130, Jul. 2016)
原文链接：https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article…

引言：当可验证的客观绩效衡量标准不完善时，委托人可以使用主观绩效薪酬。然而这给了监督者诱导员工偏离任务的权利，这更有利于监督者而不是委托人。文章依托多任务的委托-监督-代理模型，监督者对特定任务由内在的偏好，并且可能会收到关于代理人努力的软信息。研究发现，基于有偏见的监督评价的主观绩效薪酬与基于不一致绩效衡量的激励薪酬对代理人跨任务努力分配具有相同的扭曲作用。将不一致的绩效衡量与有偏见的监督相结合，可以减轻这种扭曲，但并不总是能够消除。文章将结果应用到专家型和通才型中层管理人员之间的选择上，这时出现了监督能力偏见之间的权衡。*

*中文翻译
