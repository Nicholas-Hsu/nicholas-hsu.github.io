Participate in the online seminar 5th, the following is the article basic information this time.

Paper: A Few Bad Apples Spoil the Barrel: An Anti-Folk Theorem for Anonymous Repeated Games with Incomplete Information

Authors: Takuo Sugaya; Alexander Wolitzky
Published Journal: American Economic Review (Vol. 110, No. 12, Dec. 2020)
Links of the Original Papers: https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.20200068

Abstract: We study anonymous repeated games where players may be “commitment types” who always take the same action. We establish a stark anti-folk theorem: if the distribution of the number of commitment types satisfies a smoothness condition and the game has a “pairwise dominant” action, this action is almost always taken. This implies that cooperation is impossible in the repeated prisoner’s dilemma with anonymous random matching. We also bound equilibrium payoffs for general games. Our bound implies that industry profits converge to zero in linear-demand Cournot oligopoly as the number of firms increases.

论文：《一个坏苹果，坏了一筐果: 不完全信息下匿名重复博弈中的一个反大众定理》*

作者：Takuo Sugaya; Alexander Wolitzky
发表杂志：《American Economic Review》 (Vol. 110, No. 12, Dec. 2020)
原文链接：https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.20200068

引言：我们研究的是匿名重复游戏，其中玩家可能是总是采取相同行动的“承诺类型”。我们建立了一个明显的反大众定理:如果（参与者的）承诺类型的数量分布满足一个平滑条件，并且博弈具有“成对支配”行动，则参与者几乎总是采取这个行动。这意味着在匿名随机匹配的重复囚徒困境中，合作是不可能的。我们还确定了一般博弈的均衡支付的界。在线性需求的古诺博弈中，随着企业数量的增加，这个界意味着行业利润收敛于零。*

*中文翻译
